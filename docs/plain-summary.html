The Safety-Shaped Blind Spot

Do language models notice when you inject a harmful thought into them?

Models can sometimes report a concept secretly injected into their activations. This project tests whether they go blind to it exactly when the concept is harmful — the case safety needs most.

The idea

Anthropic showed that a model's own internal representation of a word — say bread — can be added into its activations while it works on something unrelated. Often the model reports it: "I'm having an intrusive thought about bread," before it ever says the word. That is a model reading its own internal state.

The same work found that refusal training suppresses this noticing — switch refusal off and detection jumps from about 11% to 64%. Harmful concepts are exactly what triggers refusal. So the untested prediction: models should be worst at noticing the injected thoughts that are harmful.

Why it matters

A recurring hope in safety is that a model might tell us when something is wrong inside it — "I think I'm being manipulated." A blind spot shaped like harm puts a hole in that hope precisely where the stakes are highest. And it fails silently: the model does not error, it confidently says "no injection." A monitor that is reliable on bread and blind on weapon is worse than none, because it gives false assurance.

The flip side is also useful: if harmful steering is inherently harder to notice, covert auditing of models already works and is more robust than assumed.

Key terms

Activations / residual stream
A model's internal "working memory" — a vector at each word position that every layer reads and writes. Being additive, a vector can be injected into it mid-computation.
Concept injection
Adding the model's own internal direction for a word into that working memory. No new information, no capability — just a nudge to its internal state.
Introspection (here)
The model noticing and reporting the injected thought before it says the word. That ordering is what separates real self-access from just talking about the topic.
Refusal direction
Refusal is carried by a single internal direction (Arditi et al.). Present, the model refuses. Abliteration deletes it — the result is an uncensored model. A published technique, reused here rather than improved.
Cosine similarity
How aligned two directions are: 1 identical, 0 unrelated. Used here to ask whether the "weapon" concept points the same way as refusal.
TPR / FPR
True-positive rate (says "injected" when it was) reported at a false-positive rate (says "injected" when nothing was). Both are needed — a model that always says yes scores 100% but is useless.

The method

Five steps, cheapest and riskiest first

Run on Gemma-3-27B — activation injection needs open weights, and the detection effect is documented in it at a known operating point, giving a number to reproduce.

0

Premise check · do first, ~free

Does the "weapon" concept actually point the same way as refusal, at the injection layer? Never measured for concept vectors. Inference-only, runs on a small 4B model. A negative result is itself publishable and saves an expensive experiment on a broken premise.

1

Reproduce the baseline · hard gate

Re-run the original experiment and hit the published ~38% detection number. If it will not reproduce, every downstream number is uninterpretable. Nothing expensive happens until this passes.

2

Build four word lists ("arms")

The scientific core. "Harmful" carries confounds, so three controls isolate it:

Neutralbreadbaseline
Negative but harmlessgriefrules out "just unpleasant"
Harm-adjacent, harmlesssurgeryrules out "just a scary topic"
Harmfulweaponthe hypothesis
3

The main test

Inject each word, ask the model if it noticed, measure detection per arm. Also measure how often it refuses to answer — that is data, not noise: a refusal to engage is the mechanism acting, not a failure to detect.

4

The causal test

Turn off refusal (abliteration) and re-run — if refusal is the cause, the harmful-arm blind spot should close. Mandatory control: also delete a random direction. If that closes the gap too, the experiment has only shown "damaging the model changes things." Two papers disagree on how this comes out.

Headline chart: detection rate vs. how refusal-aligned each concept is. If the mechanism is real, the line slopes down, and abliteration flattens it.

Every outcome is a result

  • Blind spot, harmful arm only — self-report has a safety-shaped hole.
  • Drop in a control arm too — it is valence or topic, not harm.
  • No blind spot — confirms a competing published claim (introspection is content-independent) on the dimension most likely to break it.
  • Refusal vs. random control — resolves a live disagreement between two papers either way.

Key papers

The two the project stands on

Emergent Introspective AwarenessLindsey · Anthropic · 2025First showed models can notice injected concepts.
Mechanisms of Introspective AwarenessMacar et al. · Anthropic · arXiv:2603.21396The paper this project extends: method, test model, the 38% baseline, and the finding that refusal suppresses detection. Excluded harmful concepts by policy — that exclusion is the gap this project fills.

The mechanism, from adjacent angles

Refusal Is Mediated by a Single DirectionArditi et al. · NeurIPS 2024Refusal lives in one direction; remove it to uncensor. Source of the refusal direction and abliteration.
Safety Pitfalls of Steering VectorsLi et al. · TU Munich · arXiv:2603.24543Steering vectors partly align with the refusal direction — the other half of the prediction.
Self-Report of Adversarial PrefillsNguyen et al. · KAIST · arXiv:2606.23671Closest prior work: same refusal-suppression effect, but for text the model wrote, not activation injection.

The claim the project tests against

Introspection Is Content-AgnosticLederman & Mahowald · arXiv:2603.05414Argues detection does not depend on content. The headline result is a direct test of this.
Do LLMs Know When They'll Refuse?Gondil · arXiv:2604.00228Weapons queries are hardest for introspection; source of "measure refusal, don't assume harm labels."

Why the premise check can genuinely fail

HARC · Llorente-SaguerarXiv:2607.00572 · arXiv:2604.18901Harm and refusal are distinct directions that drift apart in deeper layers, and the recovered direction depends on extraction. Why Step 0 is a real test, not a formality.

Responsible use: the project injects concept words, not capabilities — a "weapon" direction teaches the model nothing new. The measured output is a detection rate, which is safe to share. The sensitive artifacts — the uncensored model and the concept vectors — stay on a controlled machine and are never published. A fallback design tests the same mechanism with zero harmful concepts.
